How to use Spotify in India(or any unsupported country).

Chaitanya Giri
2018-08-04
Chaitanya Giri, Proxy, Tutorial, how to, india, spotify, use

No doubt spotify is a king when it comes to the music industry. The wide variety it offers is just uncompetitive. But its biggest drawback is that it isn’t available in India. You can’t keep 17% of the total world population away from something as good as spotify.(Yes, I know not everyone has access to Internet but thats a discussion for some other day).

I was working on some project and suddenly I felt a sudden need to hear some good music to pace up my work and refresh my mood. I realized I don’t have much option in this case and every option available had some major drawbacks let me list down a few — 

  • Youtube — heavy data consumption, bad suggestion engine
  • Wynk , saavn, gaana etc.— Paid, Heavy advertisements (even in between songs), shitty collection.

So with lots of effort(Not so much) I figured out how to use spotify in a country where they don’t offer their services even without any proxy server(YES it is possible). With only minutes of research you will be able to find out that they(Spotify) do not continuously check for your location but they do it only when you login/sign up for the application so you only have to somehow bypass the authentication part and you will be good to go.

Follow these steps — 

  • Download Zenmate or any other proxy extension/addon
  • Download spotify app available for windows, linux(YES), Mac, android and ios
  • Log in to facebook using your default browser.
  • Turn on Zenmate proxy and select USA or any supported country (just few clicks in UI, you’ll have to use it just once don’t worry I keep my promises).
  • Open the app and login to the Spotify using facebook it will open your default browser in which proxy is running you can verify your account there. It’ll redirect you to the app/website.
  • Just turn off the proxy and remove extension temporarily(if you want). Boom you are done open spotify app and play whatever you want.
