Explaining Reservation in India to a complete foreigner.

Chaitanya Giri
2018-04-06
Chaitanya Giri, India, Reservation, Social Issues

Since medium tells me that I have some international readers so I thought that I should explain Indian reservation system to them. Indians(protesters) who claim to already know about it might also want to consider reading it.

http://writm.com

Indian reservation system was started way before independence (Search for British influence in India) by Maharajah of Kolhapur in 1891(supposedly) in his own reign. His 1902 measures created 50 per cent reservation for backward communities.

Q. Was reservation even required back then?

A. YES! in fact, you can’t even imagine the condition of people back then. Despite all the good things that Indian civilization possessed it had some major drawbacks, for example, Sati Pratha, Untouchability etc. While Indians were already getting harassed by Britishers the backward community’s condition was even worse some so-called upper caste people of India used their superiority in the community to harass the backward communities.

Q. What are these backward communities and how they were formed?

A. The place where I come from backward communities were usually those people who were not able to keep up with the changes in the world or better say did not have opportunities/facilities like other people. Indian caste system classified the society in principle into four varnas(types):

1, Brahmin- priests, scholars and teachers.\
2, Kshatriya- rulers, warriors and administrators.\
3, Vaishya- agriculturalists and merchants.\
4, Shudra- labourers and service providers.

Apart from these types, the unclassified ones were called avarna most of the people who have reservation nowadays belong to this category. While it may look like it is been a discriminative classification but the people who were responsible for this were the most genius brains at that time and even now. This system was robust, every part of the society had equal importance and every part of society was required to work efficiently for a community to grow.

But the influence of other civilizations like Mughals, Europeans and British made it the way it is now. There was indeed a loophole in the Indian caste system. Now instead of classifying people on the basis of the work they do they were classified on the basis of caste they were born in.

Presently reservation in India exists in employment, education and elected bodies.

Q. Is reservation required now?

A. YES! I have seen people living in worst conditions only if they had proper education, and better opportunities their lifestyles would be way better than now. But as said by many global thinkers Indian reservation system needs some serious reforms. Now, remember the caste-based system which already had loopholes, the people who drafted the Indian Constitution(I don’t think that’s just one person to be credited or blamed for this) forgot about the loophole and again took the same cast based system as a foundation to define new reservation system.
