International Men’s Day Vs International Women’s Day.

Chaitanya Giri
2017-11-20
Feminism, Men's Day, Political, Sexist, Women's Day

Image via: ebs.ie

I write mostly about tech. So whats the need to even think out of context. Trust me I won’t, if situation would not have been this serious. I may get some ** feminist comments but I think its worth taking a risk.

Do you know what day was celebrated yesterday? Come on make a guess? Most of you might have already guessed it as the heading says it all (FacePalm). Let me tell you yesterday was International Men’s Day.

I usually don’t mind much about dates(the ones in calendar of-course 😜 ). But I always believe statistics can tell a lot. So I have a habit of using Google trends I observe and analyze data to produce useful and amazing(sometimes shocking) results. So I was going through it and found plenty of things to worry about:

1, It showed “International Men’s day” was searched 15 times more on March than on November. Fun fact: International Men’s day is celebrated on 19th November and International Women’s day on 8th March. People are so ignorant that they tend to search about “Men’s day” on Women’s day rather than on Men’s day.

See the peak in March.

Women’s Day = 1, Men’s Day = 0.

2, So I went ahead and continued this interesting research a bit to find out that comparing query searches of “International Men’s Day” and “International Women’s Day” was even worse.(Google-Trends converts its data in percentage automatically.) Percentage of people who searched for “Men’s Day” was close to ZERO (0) while percentage of people who searched for “Women’s Day” was close to 100 annually.

Red = International Women’s Day ; Blue = International Men’s Day

__Red = “International Women’s Day” searches more than “International men’s day” searches. __Blue = “International Men’s Day” searches more than “International women’s day” searches.

Women’s Day = 2, Men’s Day = 0.

3, Now this one is actually funny as well as shocking. People actually searched for “Is there an International Men’s Day”. Really, who ever did that must be having an existential crisis.

Look in related query section.

Women’s Day = 3, Men’s Day = -1 (People searched “Is there an International Men’s Day”)

These are not merely stats, these are the reflection of what world has become. Instead of promoting equality among genders and implementing practical solutions people are hyping topics people are using terms like “feminism” to grind their own axes.

So what happened here is people have hyped this thing so much that it don’t even feel like equality. It feels like it has been burdened upon us. We must understand at the end of the day terms do not matter what matters is the intentions.
